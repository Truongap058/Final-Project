Batman is a fictional superhero appearing in comic books published by DC Comics. Created by artist Bob Kane and writer Bill Finger, Batman first appeared in Detective Comics #27 in 1939. The character, alter ego of billionaire Bruce Wayne, witnessed the murder of his parents as a child, leading him to dedicate his life to fighting crime in Gotham City. Batman has no superhuman powers but relies on his intellect, detective skills, physical prowess, martial arts training, and a vast array of gadgets and technology to combat criminals. He is often depicted as a brooding, complex character and is a founding member of the Justice League. Batman has become one of the most iconic and enduring figures in popular culture, appearing in various media adaptations, including films, TV series, and video games.

Superman

The Man of Steel

Superman is a fictional superhero featured in comic books published by DC Comics. Created by writer Jerry Siegel and artist Joe Shuster, Superman first appeared in Action Comics #1 in 1938. The character's alter ego is Clark Kent, a journalist who works for the Daily Planet in the fictional city of Metropolis. Superman possesses superhuman abilities, including super strength, speed, flight, invulnerability, heat vision, and X-ray vision, due to Earth's yellow sun. His origin story involves being sent to Earth from the dying planet Krypton as a baby and being raised by the Kent family. Superman is often portrayed as a symbol of hope, justice, and morality. He is a founding member of the Justice League and has become one of the most iconic and enduring superheroes in popular culture, featured in various adaptations such as films, TV shows, and animated series.

Spider-Man is a fictional superhero created by writer Stan Lee and artist Steve Ditko, first appearing in Amazing Fantasy #15 in 1962, published by Marvel Comics. The character's alter ego is Peter Parker, a high school student who gains spider-like abilities after being bitten by a radioactive spider. Peter Parker uses his newfound powers, including super strength, agility, and the ability to cling to walls, to fight crime and protect the citizens of New York City. He designed a costume for himself and created web-shooters that allow him to swing between buildings and create web-based weapons.

The Flash is a fictional superhero appearing in comic books published by DC Comics. Created by writer Gardner Fox and artist Harry Lampert, The Flash first appeared in Flash Comics #1 in 1940. The character has had several iterations, but the most well-known is Barry Allen. Barry is a forensic scientist who gains super speed after a lightning strike and a chemical accident. He uses his newfound abilities to fight crime in Central City as The Flash. The Flash is a member of the Justice League and is known for his red costume, lightning bolt symbol, and the ability to move at incredible speeds, allowing him to travel through time and vibrate through solid objects. The character has been featured in various comic book series, animated TV shows, and is part of the Arrowverse, a shared universe in live-action television.
